### 8.1 Main Conclusions

### 8.2 Limitations
Review-level limitations include the absence of eligible studies despite a comprehensive screen of 159 records, which restricted the synthesis to an evidence-gap assessment rather than quantitative pooling. Strict inclusion criteria required identical TDR liquid-formula interventions across diabetes strata, which enhances internal validity but may have excluded otherwise informative studies using partial meal replacements or food-based VLCDs. We relied on reported abstracts/titles for initial exclusion; although reasons were systematically applied, misclassification at screening is always possible. The pre-specified statistical plan listed an odds ratio as the effect measure, which is not optimal for continuous weight outcomes; future protocols should specify mean differences (kg) and rates (kg/week).

Study-level limitations reflect the state of the literature rather than decisions in this review. Many trials focus on T2DM only or non-diabetes populations only, do not stratify outcomes by diabetes status, or incorporate unequal co-interventions (pharmacologic agents, exercise, or behavioral programs) that confound weight loss. Reporting often emphasizes percent weight loss or categorical thresholds without providing absolute kg and weekly rates with variance estimates. Follow-up durations and maintenance strategies vary widely, and medication adjustments for T2DM are inconsistently standardized and reported, further limiting comparability.

### 8.3 Implications
For practice:
- There is no direct comparative evidence to support assumptions that weight loss under identical TDR liquid-formula programs differs between people with and without T2DM. Clinicians should not presume faster or slower weight loss by diabetes status on this basis. If TDR is used, clinical decisions should be individualized, with careful monitoring and standardized medication adjustment in patients with T2DM to ensure safety, but expectations about differential weight-loss magnitude or rate should remain cautious given the lack of comparative data.

For research:
- Priority should be given to randomized controlled trials or well-designed prospective cohorts that enroll both T2DM and non‑T2DM participants and deliver the same TDR liquid-formula protocol (e.g., <800 vs >800 kcal/day), with identical maintenance and behavioral support. Studies should pre-specify and report absolute final weight change (kg), weekly rate (kg/week), and dispersion (SD/SE), along with adherence, attrition, and medication changes. To detect clinically plausible differences, sample sizes should be powered for small between-group differences (e.g., ~2–3 kg at 8–12 weeks or ~0.2–0.3 kg/week), adjusted for baseline BMI, age, sex, and insulin use. Stratified analyses by insulin therapy and diabetes duration are essential, and medication adjustment algorithms should be standardized and reported. Protocol registration, harmonized outcome definitions, and sharing of individual participant data would enable robust meta-analyses to definitively address whether diabetes status modifies weight-loss response to TDR.
