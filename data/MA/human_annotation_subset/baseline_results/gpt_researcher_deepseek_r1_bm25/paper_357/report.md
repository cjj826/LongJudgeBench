## Systematic Review of Postmortem Evidence for Cerebral Inflammation in Schizophrenia: Neuroinflammatory Markers in Human Brain Tissue

### Introduction  
Schizophrenia is a severe psychiatric disorder characterized by heterogeneous symptomatology, with emerging evidence supporting neuroimmune dysregulation as a potential pathophysiological mechanism. This systematic review synthesizes postmortem evidence evaluating neuroinflammatory markers—including microglial activation, astrocytic alterations, cytokine dysregulation, and associated inflammatory mediators—in schizophrenia patients compared to healthy controls. The analysis focuses exclusively on human postmortem brain studies to elucidate central nervous system-specific inflammatory processes, avoiding confounding factors from peripheral measures ([Glial Cell Abnormalities in Major Psychiatric Diseases, 2022](source_url)).

### Methods  
#### Search Strategy and Inclusion Criteria  
This review incorporated studies from a comprehensive corpus (N=58 articles) satisfying:  
1. **Population**: Schizophrenia patients (any subtype/phase) vs. healthy controls  
2. **Outcomes**: Quantification of microglia, astrocytes, cytokines, or related neuroinflammatory markers in postmortem brain tissue  
3. **Design**: Case-control, cohort, or meta-analyses of postmortem samples  
4. **Exclusion**: Animal models, *in vivo* imaging without postmortem correlation, or retracted publications (e.g., Rao et al., 2013)  

#### Data Extraction and Synthesis  
Data were organized by marker type (microglia, astrocytes, cytokines). Consistency of findings was assessed across brain regions, with attention to methodological rigor (e.g., stereology vs. immunoassay). Effect sizes, cohort demographics, and medication confounds were tabulated where available.

### Results  

#### Microglial Alterations  
Microglia, the CNS’s primary immune cells, exhibit **regional and state-dependent activation** in schizophrenia:  

| **Study** | **Brain Region** | **Key Finding** | **Effect Size/Notes** |  
|-----------|------------------|------------------|----------------------|  
| Steiner et al. (2006) | DLPFC, ACC, hippocampus | ↑ HLA-DR+ microglia density (↑28% in DLPFC) | Lateralization deficits in schizophrenia patients |  
| Fillman et al. (2013) | DLPFC | ↑ MHC-II+ microglia density | Correlated with cytokine mRNA elevation |  
| Trepanier et al. (2016) | White matter | ↑ Microglial activation | Ultrastructural dystrophy in oligodendrocytes |  
| Hammond et al. (2020) | Midbrain | ↑ CD163+ macrophages & C1qA/C3/C4 transcripts | "High inflammatory" subgroup identified |  
| Glial Markers of Suicidal Behavior (2024) | Multiple | Microglial activation in suicidal schizophrenia | Association with affective comorbidity |  

- **Lateralization Abnormalities**: Microglial density increases are left-hemisphere predominant in dorsolateral prefrontal cortex (DLPFC), correlating with disrupted cortical asymmetry ([Steiner et al., 2006](source_url)).  
- **Subgroup Heterogeneity**: Approximately 50% of patients show elevated microglial markers, defining a "high inflammatory biotype" linked to severe negative symptoms ([Hammond et al., 2020](source_url)).  

#### Astrocytic Pathology  
Astrocytes demonstrate **reduced density but morphological activation**, indicating functional dysregulation:  

| **Study** | **Brain Region** | **Key Finding** | **Effect Size/Notes** |  
|-----------|------------------|------------------|----------------------|  
| Trépanier et al. (2014) | DLPFC | ↑ GFAP mRNA/protein; reactive morphology | Inflammatory subgroup only |  
| Glial Review (2022) | Prefrontal cortex | ↓ Astrocyte density (11/20 studies) | Meta-analysis: ↓ S100B, connexin-43 |  
| S100 Meta-analysis (2023) | Cortex/hippocampus | ↑ S100A8/A9/A12 expression | ANXA3 co-upregulation (r=0.72) |  
| Glial Markers (2024) | Hippocampus | Astrocyte loss in suicidal patients | Affective disorder comorbidity |  

- **Inflammatory Subgroups**: Astrocytic GFAP overexpression coincides with cytokine elevations (IL-6, IL-1β), supporting neuroinflammation-associated schizophrenia subtypes ([Trépanier et al., 2014](source_url)).  
- **S100 Proteins**: Upregulated S100 genes (e.g., S100A8, S100A12) promote astrocyte-mediated neuroinflammation and correlate with Annexin A3 (ANXA3), a phospholipid-binding protein implicated in immune responses ([S100 Meta-analysis, 2023](source_url)).  

#### Cytokine and Inflammatory Mediators  
**Pro-inflammatory cytokines** and **kynurenine pathway metabolites** are consistently altered:  

| **Marker** | **Direction of Change** | **Brain Region** | **Functional Implications** |  
|------------|------------------------|-----------------|------------------------------|  
| IL-6, IL-1β | ↑ Protein/mRNA | DLPFC, midbrain | Synaptic pruning deficits; NMDA dysfunction |  
| Quinolinic acid (QA) | ↑ Concentration | Hippocampus | Neurotoxicity; correlates with cognitive deficits |  
| Kynurenic acid (KYNA) | ↑ Concentration | Prefrontal cortex | Glutamate modulation; ↑ in "high inflammation" subgroup |  
| C1qA/C3/C4 | ↑ Transcripts | Midbrain | Complement-mediated synaptic elimination |
