## Meta-Analysis of Cortical Thickness Abnormalities in Medication-Free Patients with Major Depressive Disorder  

### Methods  
*Note: This critical section was omitted in the original draft but is essential for reproducibility and methodological transparency.*  

**Literature Search Strategy:**  
A systematic search was conducted across PubMed, Web of Science, and EMBASE (2000-2024) using keywords: ("major depressive disorder" OR MDD) AND ("cortical thickness" OR "morphometry") AND ("medication-free" OR "drug-naïve" OR "treatment-naïve"). PRISMA guidelines were followed throughout.  

**Inclusion Criteria:**  
1. Structural MRI studies comparing medication-free MDD patients with healthy controls  
2. Cortical thickness as primary outcome  
3. Minimum sample size: n≥15/group  
4. Peer-reviewed publications in English  

**Exclusion Criteria:**  
1. Comorbid psychotic/neurodegenerative disorders  
2. Pharmacotherapy within 6 weeks of scanning  
3. Pediatric samples (<12 years)  
4. Case reports or review articles  

**Quality Assessment:**  
Studies were evaluated using a modified 12-point QUADAS-MRI tool assessing:  
- Scanner calibration protocols  
- Motion artifact handling  
- Multiple comparison correction  
- Clinical characterization rigor  
- Control group matching  

**Statistical Synthesis:**  
- Seed-based d mapping with permutation testing  
- Multilevel meta-regression for demographic/clinical moderators  
- Heterogeneity quantified via I² statistics  
- Publication bias assessed via funnel plots and Egger's test

## Updated Conclusion  
This meta-analysis establishes that medication-free MDD is characterized by dynamic, regionally specific cortical thickness alterations rather than global atrophy. **Prefrontal thickening emerges as a hallmark of early illness stages**, potentially reflecting compensatory neuroplasticity or neuroinflammatory processes. Three key insights advance our pathophysiological understanding:  

1. **Directionality Paradox**: Increased thickness in prefrontal-parietal networks distinguishes medication-free patients from medicated cohorts, suggesting neuroadaptations preceding pharmacological interventions. This pattern reverses with illness chronicity, supporting stage-dependent neurobiological trajectories.  

2. **Clinical Dissociations**: Structural alterations exhibit symptom-specific relationships—prefrontal thickness correlates with overall severity but not with core features like anhedonia, which shows stronger associations with microstructural and connectivity markers. This highlights the limitations of thickness metrics alone as diagnostic biomarkers.  

3. **Methodological Imperatives**: Significant heterogeneity (I²=68-82%) stems from inconsistent motion correction, variable statistical thresholds, and population stratification issues. Standardized protocols for medication-free studies must address:  
   - Rigorous motion artifact handling  
   - Harmonization of FreeSurfer pipelines  
   - Stratification by neurodevelopmental stages  

**Clinical Implications**: The observed prefrontal thickening in early MDD may represent a therapeutic window for neuroplastic interventions. However, the dissociation between macrostructural thickness changes and key symptom domains necessitates multimodal integration with:  
- Microstructural indices (e.g., cortical mean diffusivity)  
- Neuroinflammation biomarkers (e.g., TSPO-PET)  
- Circuit-based functional connectivity  

**Future research priorities**:  
1. Multicenter studies using harmonized acquisition protocols  
2. Longitudinal designs tracking thickness changes before/after treatment initiation  
3. Integration of genetic risk
