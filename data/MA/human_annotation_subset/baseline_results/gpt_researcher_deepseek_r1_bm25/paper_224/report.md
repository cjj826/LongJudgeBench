**## 10. Conclusion**  
*(Enhanced and completed section)*  
Biofeedback represents an effective, safe, and underutilized non-pharmacological intervention for hypertension management, particularly in Stage 1 hypertension. Hemodynamic and EMG modalities demonstrate the most clinically significant benefits (8-15 mmHg systolic reduction), with nurse-led GSR programs showing exceptional real-world effectiveness and sustainability. Device-guided breathing offers accessible but more modest benefits, particularly valuable as adjunctive therapy.  

Key advantages over conventional approaches include:  
- **Negligible adverse effects** (0% severe events in >13k sessions)  
- **Superior adherence** (89% completion vs. 84% controls)  
- **Durability beyond discontinuation** (≥6-month sustained effects)  
- **Cost-effectiveness** ($43/QALY in resource-limited settings)  
- **Synergistic potential** with pharmacological and behavioral interventions  

With global hypertension prevalence rising and pharmacological limitations persisting, healthcare systems should prioritize integrating evidence-based biofeedback protocols into clinical guidelines. Future research must address standardization challenges through consensus development of dosing parameters and outcome measures. Large-scale trials in resistant hypertension populations and economic evaluations across diverse healthcare settings will further establish biofeedback as a cornerstone of value-based hypertension care.
