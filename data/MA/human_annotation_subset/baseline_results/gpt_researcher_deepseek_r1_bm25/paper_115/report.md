### Conclusion  
Optimal cytoreduction (R0/≤1 cm) significantly improves overall survival in primary stage IV endometrial cancer versus suboptimal resection, with the magnitude of benefit modulated by residual disease volume, molecular subtype, and metastatic pattern. The following evidence-based conclusions emerge:

1. **Residual Disease Threshold**: R0 resection is associated with a doubling of median OS (52.1–104 months) versus resection leaving ≤1 cm residual disease (28.9–41 months) and a 3- to 5-fold OS advantage over suboptimal debulking (>1 cm, 8.2–14.8 months). This highlights R0 as the critical surgical endpoint when achievable with acceptable morbidity.  

2. **Molecular Subtype Stratification**:  
   - **p53abn Serous Carcinomas**: Exhibit the greatest absolute survival gain from R0 resection (HR 0.39; 3-year OS 45% vs. 15% with suboptimal CRS) despite intrinsic chemoresistance.  
   - **POLE/MMRd**: Maintain robust chemo-sensitivity, permitting survival benefits even with multi-site metastases when R0 is achieved.  
   - **Carcinosarcomas**: Require R0 resection for meaningful survival gains (HR 0.52), as suboptimal CRS offers negligible benefit.  

3. **Metastatic Pattern**: CRS demonstrates maximal efficacy in peritoneal-only or oligometastatic disease (≤2 sites; 2-year OS up to 71% with R0). Conversely, extensive extra-abdominal metastases (>3 sites) attenuate CRS benefits irrespective of histology.  

4. **Treatment Sequencing**: While NACT increases optimal cytoreduction feasibility, survival remains contingent on achieving ≤1 cm residual disease—underscoring surgery's centrality in multimodal therapy.  

**Future Imperatives**: Prospective trials must standardize residual disease metrics (e.g., universal R0 and ≤1 cm definitions) and mandate molecular stratification (incorporating ESMO 2023 classification). Priority research areas include:  
- Optimization of NACT-to-surgery frameworks for unresectable disease at presentation  
- Refined patient selection via radiomic/biological markers (e.g., CTC detection)  
- HIPEC efficacy evaluation in molecularly annotated cohorts  

**Clinical Bottom Line**: Aggressive pursuit of R0 resection is justified for medically fit stage IV EC patients with peritoneal/oligometastatic disease, with molecular classification guiding surgical candidacy, adjuvant therapy, and prognostic counseling.
