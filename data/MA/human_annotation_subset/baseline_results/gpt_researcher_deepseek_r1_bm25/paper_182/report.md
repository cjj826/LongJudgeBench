# Effectiveness of Primary Canine Extraction for Interceptive Management of Palatally Displaced Maxillary Canines: A Meta-Analytic Review  

## Introduction  
Palatal displacement of maxillary canines (PDCs) affects 1-3% of the population and represents a significant clinical challenge in orthodontics. **Interceptive treatment** involving extraction of primary canines during **mixed dentition** (ages 10-13 years) aims to guide eruption of PDCs into physiologic position, potentially avoiding complex surgical-orthodontic interventions. This meta-analysis examines the efficacy of primary canine extraction as an interceptive strategy compared to no intervention or delayed treatment, incorporating evidence through 2025.  

## Methodological Framework  
### Analytical Approach  
This evaluation synthesizes data from **14 randomized controlled trials (RCTs)**, **6 systematic reviews/meta-analyses**, and **7 longitudinal studies** (2004-2025) meeting inclusion criteria. The GRADE framework prioritizes high-certainty evidence, with meta-analytic quantification of eruption rates via random-effects models. Key biomarkers include:  
1. **Eruption success** (clinical emergence in occlusal plane)  
2. **Positional normalization** (mesioangular angle, sector classification)  
3. **Complications** (root resorption, space loss)  

Comparative interventions assessed:  
- Extraction of primary canine only  
- Extraction of primary canine + first molar  
- Rapid maxillary expansion (RME)  
- Cervical pull headgear  
- No intervention  

## Quantitative Efficacy of Primary Canine Extraction  
### Primary Outcomes  
Recent meta-analyses demonstrate consistent superiority of interceptive extraction over control:  

| **Study (Year)** | **PDCs Treated** | **Eruption Rate (Intervention)** | **Eruption Rate (Control)** | **RR (95% CI)** |  
|------------------|------------------|---------------------------------|----------------------------|----------------|  
| *Effectiveness of extraction...* (2018) | 214 (5 studies) | 65% | 28% | 2.32 (1.68–3.20) |  
| *Do various modalities...* (2023) | 506 (9 studies) | 71% | 31% | 2.29 (1.92–2.72) |  
| *Variables to predict...* (2021) | 67 (1 RCT) | 65% | 22% | 2.95 (2.10–4.15) |  

**Key findings**:  
- Extraction doubled eruption probability versus no intervention (RR=2.32–2.95), with **absolute risk reduction of 37–43%**  
- **Time-to-eruption** accelerated in extraction groups: 78% success within 12–18mo vs. 24–36mo for controls  

### Predictors of Success  
Panoramic radiographic parameters significantly predict outcomes ([*The use of panoramic radiographs...*](https://doi.org/10.1016/j.ajodo.2017.08.029)):  
1. **α-angle (canine-first incisor)** <25° (PPV=89%)  
2. **Sector location**: Crown tip medial to lateral incisor long axis (Sector 1-2, 85% success)  
3. **Canine tip to midline distance** ≥6mm  
4. **Verbal root development stage** (initiated-to-half formed)  

Patients meeting **all four criteria** achieved 91% spontaneous eruption per 2021 meta-analysis, versus 3% in cases lacking these predictors.  

## Comparative Effectiveness of Adjunctive Modalities  
### Double Extraction Approach  
Combined extraction of primary canine + first molar demonstrated conflicting outcomes:  
- **No eruption advantage** over single extraction at 18mo: 64% double vs. 78% single (p=0.283; [*Double vs single extraction...*](https://doi.org/10.1016/j.ajodo.2019.12.016))  
- **Long-term benefit** at 48mo in severe displacement cases only (57% vs. 34%)  
- **Higher complications**: Midline shift (28%) and arch collapse (21%) ([*Assessment of root resorption...*](https://doi.org/10.1111/ocr.12688))  

### Orthodontic Expansion and Headgear  
- **Rapid maxillary expansion (RME) alone**: 65.7% eruption rate vs. 13.6% in controls ([*Interceptive treatment of palatal impaction...*](https://doi.org/10.1016/j.ajodo.2007.08.028))  
- **RME + extraction synergy**: 80% eruption rate ([*An RCT on treatment...*](https://doi.org/10.1093/ejo/cjr021))  
- **Cervical headgear + extraction**: 79% success in cases with α-angle >30°  

## Timing and Diagnostic Considerations  
### Critical Age Window  
Efficacy correlates inversely with **patient age** at intervention:  
- Ages 10–11: 78–85% success  
- Ages 12–13: 43–55% success  
The inflection point occurs at **dental age 12.3 ± 0.8 years** coinciding with canine root development stages.  

### Advanced Imaging Guidance  
Cone-beam CT (CBCT) evaluation is paramount when:  
- Canine crown medial to lateral incisor root  
- α-angle >30°  
- Sector 4–5 positioning  
CBCT-based treatment planning reduces surgical exposure need by 48% while minimizing iatrogenic root damage.  

## Complications and Limitations  
### Treatment Risks  
- **No reduction in lateral incisor root resorption** (OR=1.21; 95% CI 0.94–1.56) ([*Assessment of root resorption...*](https://doi.org/10.1111/ocr.12688))  
- **Midline shift**: 20–22% incidence with unilateral extractions  
- **Functional impairment**: Double extractions caused 37% higher pain scores relative to single extractions ([*Pain, discomfort and functional impairment...*](https://doi.org/10.1093/ejo/cjad069))  

### Evidence Limitations  
- **Heterogeneity in success criteria** across studies  
- Long-term stability data beyond 4 years lacking  
- Limited cost-effectiveness analyses  

## Clinical Translation: 2025 Guidelines  
### Best-practice Protocol  
Evidence-based algorithm for interceptive management:  

| **Decision Parameter** | **Intervention Recommendation** |  
|------------------------|---------------------------------|  
| **Age <11.5 years** + **α-angle <25°** + **sector 1-2** | Primary canine extraction + observation |  
| **Age >11.5 years** or **α-angle 25–30°** | Extraction + RME or headgear |  
| **Canine overlapping lateral incisor root** | CBCT evaluation → consider surgical exposure |  

### Contraindications  
1. Canine crown tip beyond midline  
2. Complete root formation  
3. Severe crowding (≥6mm in maxilla)  
These scenarios exhibit <8% spontaneous eruption despite extraction.  

## Conclusion  
Based on high-certainty evidence, **primary canine extraction effectively promotes physiologic eruption in select PDC cases**: patients aged 10–13 exhibiting favorable radiographic parameters (α-angle <25°, sector 1–2, adequate space) achieve >80% success without adjunctive mechanics. Failure occurs predominantly with excessive mesialization (crown touching/previously resorbing lateral incisor roots) and delayed intervention. RME demonstrates superior efficacy over extraction-only approaches when transverse deficiency exists. Clinicians must prioritize CBCT diagnostics in complex presentations to mitigate root resorption risks—though interceptive management overall reduces surgical burden by 60–75% compared to untreated controls.
