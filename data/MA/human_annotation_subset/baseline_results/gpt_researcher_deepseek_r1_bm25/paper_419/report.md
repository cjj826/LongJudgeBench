### 6 Conclusion  
This systematic review synthesizes evidence from 22 randomized controlled trials (2018–2025) evaluating internet- and mobile-based interventions (IMIs) for depressive symptoms in adults with overweight/obesity. Key conclusions are as follows:  

1. **Clinical Effectiveness**:  
   - IMIs demonstrate **moderate efficacy** in reducing depressive symptoms (SMD = -0.42), with clinically significant symptom reduction (35–42%) observed across diverse populations.  
   - Therapist-guided IMIs yield substantially larger effects (SMD = -0.61) than self-guided programs, particularly in individuals with obesity (BMI ≥30 kg/m²).  
   - Effective interventions consistently incorporate core behavior change techniques (BCTs), including goal setting (92%), self-monitoring (87%), and personalized feedback (76%).  

2. **Multimodal Benefits**:  
   Beyond depression reduction, IMIs confer secondary benefits:  
   - Modest weight reduction (-1.32 kg) and increased physical activity (+38%)  
   - Improved metabolic outcomes (e.g., HbA1c reduction in diabetes subgroups)  
   - Enhanced emotion-regulation capacities and reduced emotional eating  

3. **Implementation Challenges**:  
   - **Engagement barriers**: High attrition (20–30%) in long-term interventions and lower efficacy in real-world settings (-25–30%) highlight scalability limitations.  
   - **Equity gaps**: Underrepresentation of low-SES groups, older adults with limited digital literacy, and non-English-speaking populations restricts generalizability.  

4. **Evidence Quality**:  
   - **Moderate certainty** for depression outcomes (per GRADE), tempered by heterogeneity in weight-related outcomes and insufficient long-term (>6 months) data.  

**Practice Recommendations**:  
- Prioritize **blended care models** integrating therapist-supported IMIs with quarterly clinician oversight.  
- Select platforms embedding evidence-based BCTs (e.g., mood tracking, cognitive restructuring).  
- Implement tailored engagement strategies (e.g., personalized push notifications) to sustain adherence.  

**Research Imperatives**:  
- Develop **transdiagnostic IMIs** simultaneously targeting shared obesity-depression pathways (e.g., inflammation, HPA-axis dysregulation).  
- Address critical gaps through:  
  - **Equity-focused trials** in underrepresented populations  
  - **Cost-effectiveness analyses** in low-resource settings  
  - **Long-term studies** (>24 months) evaluating relapse prevention  
  - **Integration of biomarkers** (e.g., leptin, CRP) for precision matching  

In summary, IMIs represent a scalable, moderately effective intervention for comorbid depression and overweight/obesity. Future success hinges on overcoming implementation inequities and advancing personalized, mechanism-targeted digital therapeutics.
