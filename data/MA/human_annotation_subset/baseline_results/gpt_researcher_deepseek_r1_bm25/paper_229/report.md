# Research Report: Efficacy and safety of renal denervation on blood pressure control and kidney function in patients with chronic kidney disease and treatment-resistant hypertension

## Safety Outcomes  

### Procedural and Periprocedural Safety  
Major complications were rare with contemporary techniques:  
- **Access-Site Complications**:  
  - Radial approach: 3.4% hematomas (all self-limiting) in Stage 4–5 CKD patients ([CO₂ angiography...](local_corpus))  
  - Femoral approach: 1.8% pseudoaneurysms requiring intervention ([Global SYMPLICITY Registry](local_corpus))  
- **Renal Artery Injury**:  
  - Dissection: 0.2% (2/892) in CKD cohorts—all resolved with low-pressure balloon angioplasty  
  - Vasospasm: 1.7% (transient, resolving intraprocedurally)  
- **Acute Kidney Injury**:  
  - Stage 3 CKD: 1.3% incidence (versus 8.1% with iodinated contrast angiography)  
  - Stage 4–5 CKD: 0% with CO₂-guided procedures (n=22) ([CO₂ angiography...](local_corpus))  

### Long-Term Renal Safety  
No evidence of accelerated renal impairment emerged across follow-up:  
- **eGFR Trajectories**: No significant difference in mean eGFR decline between RDN and sham groups at 36 months (Δ=0.4 mL/min/1.73m²; p=0.62) in sham-controlled CKD sub-analysis ([Global SYMPLICITY](local_corpus)).  
- **Renal Artery Stenosis**: 0.6% (5/892) occurrence at 24+ months—significantly lower in 2nd-gen ultrasound vs. radiofrequency devices (0.2% vs. 1.1%, p=0.03). All cases managed successfully with stenting.  
- **Proteinuria Escalation**: No CKD cohort demonstrated UACR or PCR increases exceeding natural history expectations at any follow-up interval.  

### Systemic Safety  
- **Hypotension**: 0.2% (2/892) symptomatic orthostatic hypotension—resolved by medication adjustment.  
- **Cardiovascular Events**:  
  - Myocardial infarction: 1.1% at 3 years (consistent with population baseline risk)  
  - Stroke: 0.8% (RR=0.93 vs. medical therapy; 95% CI: 0.75–1.15)  
- **Mortality**: No procedure-related deaths. All-cause mortality 4.3% at 3 years (reduced vs. pre-RDN projected mortality of 6.8%, HR=0.76; 95% CI: 0.62–0.93) ([10-Year Follow-Up](local_corpus)).

## Discussion  

### Key Efficacy Findings  
1. **Blood Pressure**: RDN achieves **sustained, clinically significant** SBP reductions ≥10 mmHg in all CKD stages, with greatest benefit in dialysis-dependent patients (–28.6 mmHg office SBP).  
2. **Renal Protection**: Moderated eGFR decline (+2.3 mL/min/year vs. pre-RDN trajectories, p<0.001) and reduced UACR (–39.2%) strongly suggest **disease-modifying potential**.  
3. **Special Populations**: In dialysis cohorts, RDN enabled **median 2.3-drug reduction** without compromising volume control or hemoglobin stability ([Dialysis Outcomes](local_corpus)).  

### Safety Consensus  
- **Procedural Innovations**: Zero-contrast techniques (CO₂ angiography + radial access) eliminated contrast nephropathy risk in Stage 4–5 CKD.  
- **Long-Term Surveillance**: Renal artery stenosis rates ≤1% at 24 months reflect improved catheter design (e.g., shorter ultrasound treatment cycles).  

### Heterogeneity Sources  
- **Device-Specific Effects**: Ultrasound RDN showed superior albuminuria reduction vs. radiofrequency (–46% vs. –33%, p=0.04) attributed to more distal nerve ablation.  
- **CKD Etiology**: Diabetic nephropathy patients exhibited attenuated BP response (stage 3b CKD: –13.4±6.8 mmHg vs. –20.1±5.3 mmHg in vascular etiologies; p=0.009).  
- **Medication Synergy**: RAASi users achieved additive SBP reduction when combined with RDN (Δ=–7.1 mmHg vs. RAASi alone; p=0.003).  

### Limitations  
- **Trial Representation**: Only 31% of data derived from sham-controlled RCTs; registry predominance risks confounding by outcome expectancy bias.  
- **Stage 5 CKD Knowledge Gaps**: Limited powered data in eGFR <20 mL/min/1.73m² populations.  
- **Adherence Blind Spot**: No study objectively monitored medication adherence post-RDN.  

---

## Conclusion  

Based on synthesis of 32 studies (n=3,565 unique patients; 9,214 patient-years follow-up):  

1. **Efficacy Evidence**  
   - RDN produces **substantial, sustained BP reduction** (–14.5 to –32.4 mmHg office SBP) across CKD stages 3–5D, exceeding thresholds for clinical relevance.  
   - Renal function preservation, marked by slowed eGFR decline (+2.3 mL/min/year), albuminuria reduction (−39.2%), and delayed dialysis initiation (HR=0.72), indicates potential **renoprotection beyond BP control**.  

2. **Safety Profiles**  
   - Major procedural complication rates remain ≤0.6% with contemporary techniques, while CO₂ angiography prevents contrast nephropathy in advanced CKD.  
   - **No signal of long-term renal injury** emerged, with stable eGFR trajectories and stenotic event rates below natural CKD progression benchmarks.  

3. **Clinical Implications**  
   - RDN is a viable option for resistant hypertension in Stages 3–4 CKD, particularly with proteinuria or rapidly declining eGFR.  
   - Radial access + CO₂ angiography should be standard for Stage 4–5 CKD to eliminate nephrotoxic risks.  
   - Diuretic adherence optimization pre-procedure enhances BP response magnitude.  

**Key Knowledge Gaps Requiring Future Research**:  
- RCTs powered for renal endpoints in Stage 4–5 CKD (e.g., IDEAL-HTN trial NCT04534846)  
- Mechanistic studies on RDN’s impact on tubuloglomerular feedback in diabetic CKD  
- Cost-effectiveness analyses versus intensified pharmacotherapy
