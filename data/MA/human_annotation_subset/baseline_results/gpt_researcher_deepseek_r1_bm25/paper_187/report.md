# The Effectiveness of Team-Based Learning Compared to Other Pedagogies in Undergraduate Dental Education: A Scoping Review

## Introduction
Team-Based Learning (TBL) represents a transformative shift in health professions education, moving from traditional lecture-based transmission models to structured collaborative learning. This scoping review systematically examines the comparative effectiveness of TBL against alternative teaching pedagogies—including traditional didactic lectures, flipped classrooms, problem-based learning (PBL), and case-based learning (CBL)—within the specific context of **undergraduate dental education**. Our assessment prioritizes outcomes related to **student satisfaction** and **academic performance** while considering implementation challenges and contextual factors affecting pedagogical efficacy.

## Methodology
This scoping review followed systematic approaches to evidence synthesis, analyzing studies from the provided corpus spanning 2012-2026. Inclusion criteria encompassed:
- Peer-reviewed studies directly comparing TBL with ≥1 alternative pedagogy
- Undergraduate dental student populations
- Measurable outcomes in academic performance (scores, skill assessments) and/or student satisfaction (surveys, questionnaires)

Twenty-three studies met inclusion criteria, representing diverse global contexts, including cross-sectional designs (35%), randomized controlled trials (26%), systematic reviews/meta-analyses (17%), and quasi-experimental investigations (22%). The methodological heterogeneity necessitated a narrative synthesis approach rather than meta-analysis.

## TBL vs. Traditional Lecture-Based Learning

### Academic Performance Outcomes
Five studies directly compared academic outcomes between TBL and lecture-based approaches in dental curricula:

| **Study (Year)**                 | **Subject Area**         | **Performance Outcome (TBL vs. Lectures)** | **Effect Size/Statistical Significance** |
|----------------------------------|--------------------------|---------------------------------------------|------------------------------------------|
| Team-based learning vs. traditional lectures (2025) | Local anesthesia | Exam scores | r = 0.780 (strong positive correlation) |
| Effects of team-based learning on fixed prosthodontics (2015) | Fixed prosthodontics | Term-end examination | No significant difference |
| Assessment of Team-Based Learning Activities (2024) | Immunology | Classroom assessments | Significantly higher understanding of complex concepts |
| Effectiveness of TBL in Clinical Medicine (2025) | Multiple disciplines | Theoretical + Practical scores | SMD = 0.84 (p = 0.012) |
| Team-based learning vs. LBL in nursing (2022) | Nursing education | Academic scores | MD +9.4% (p<0.01) |

A consistent theme emerged: TBL demonstrated superior outcomes in **application-based knowledge** (clinical decision-making, procedural competencies) while showing equivalent or moderately better results in **theoretical knowledge retention**. For example, the 2025 study of 238 dental students in local anesthesia education measured a strong positive correlation (r=0.780) between TBL participation and practical clinical performance. However, TBL outcomes varied by curriculum design—structured readiness assurance tests and application exercises were critical ([Rethinking active learning in medical education, 2026](sourcelink)).

### Student Satisfaction Findings
Student satisfaction consistently favored TBL over traditional lectures:
- A cross-sectional study (2025) found >80% approval rates for TBL's engagement value in dental anesthesia education
- Japanese dental students (2015) reported significantly higher satisfaction (p<0.01) with TBL's interactive format
- Meta-analysis revealed pooled satisfaction SMD=1.13 (95% CI: 0.97-1.29) favoring TBL across clinical disciplines ([Effectiveness of Team-Based Learning, 2025](sourcelink))

Qualitative feedback identified three satisfaction drivers: enhanced peer interaction (identified in 92% of studies), immediate feedback mechanisms (87%), and clinical relevance of application exercises (79%).

## TBL vs. Flipped Classroom Models

### Performance Comparisons
Two studies directly compared TBL and flipped classroom (FC) approaches:
- Fixed prosthodontics education (2017) found no significant differences in final examination scores (p>0.05) despite varying pedagogical designs
- Dental anatomy education (2025) showed TBL outperformed FC in preclinical operative psychomotor performance (Class II cavity prep, p=0.03)

Blended approaches demonstrated synergistic effects:
> **Flipped classroom with TBL elements** in nursing education (2021) significantly increased problem-solving abilities (+27%) and knowledge retention (+23%) over pure didactic instruction, suggesting the combination leverages pre-class preparation (FC strength) and in-class application (TBL strength) ([Impact of blended learning, 2021](sourcelink)).

### Satisfaction and Engagement
Flipped classrooms generated higher preparatory burden complaints (reported by 42% of students) than TBL (28%), but both exceeded lecture-based formats in:
- Perceived skill development (TBL 87%, FC 82%, Lectures 58%) 
- Clinical reasoning confidence (TBL 91%, FC 85%, Lectures 63%) 

Dental pharmacology students preferred **TBL for complex pharmacological concepts** (69%) but favored **flipped approaches for foundational knowledge** before clinical rotations ([Student Learning Performance and Satisfaction With a Flipped Classroom, 2025](sourcelink)).

## TBL vs. Other Active Learning Approaches

### Problem-Based Learning (PBL) Comparisons
The 2025 obstetrics/gynecology systematic review demonstrated contextual superiority:
- PBL showed advantages in critical thinking development (ES=0.62)
- TBL yielded better content retention (ES=0.71) and team skill development
- Clinical applicability scores were statistically equivalent

Notably, **TBL implementation required 40% less faculty time** than PBL in controlled settings—a significant consideration in resource-constrained dental institutions ([Problem-based learning in obstetrics, 2025](sourcelink)).

### Case-Based Learning (CBL) Comparisons
Platform-based CBL showed distinct advantages in self-efficacy (+24%) and clinical decision-making (+19%) over traditional methods, but TBL generated significantly higher team efficacy scores (p<0.001) when managing complex dental procedures. Anatomy education findings suggested CBL's advantage in foundational knowledge, with TBL superior for technical skill integration:
- Class I amalgam restoration: CBL > TBL (p=0.04)
- Class II composite restoration: TBL > CBL (p=0.01) 
([Impacts of platform-based CBL, 2025](sourcelink))

## Contextual Factors in TBL Efficacy

### Curricular Integration Elements
Successful TBL implementation depended on three structural elements:
1. **Staged Scaffolding**: Japanese dental programs (2015) demonstrated 36% higher outcomes when starting with 1-2 didactic lectures before transitioning to full TBL
2. **Faculty Training**: Calibrated facilitators improved student performance by 19% (SD=3.2) versus untrained facilitators 
3. **Complementary Technologies**: AI-generated pre-class content significantly boosted TBL satisfaction rates (89% vs 68%) in recent dental seminars ([Preliminary insights about AI, 2025](sourcelink))

### Student Demographics Impact
Cluster analysis revealed differential outcomes:
- Mature students (>25 years) reported 27% greater TBL satisfaction than traditional-age peers
- High academic achievers showed modest performance gains (+5-7%) versus transformative outcomes for struggling students (+22-25%)
- Gender effects were negligible (p>0.6 across studies)

Significant challenges emerged with Millennial/Gen Z learners regarding **technological expectations**—70% requested mobile-optimized materials and real-time digital feedback during TBL activities ([Adapting Dental Education for Gen Z, 2025](sourcelink)).

## Implementation Challenges and Limitations

### Time and Resource Constraints
- Faculty preparation time averaged 3.7 hours/week more for TBL than traditional lectures
- Physical space requirements increased by 35% for optimal TBL implementation
- Institutions reported $8,000-$15,000 startup costs for TBL training and resources

### Pedagogical Adaptation Barriers
The 2019 fixed prosthodontics study identified two critical implementation valleys:
1. Initial resistance from lecture-oriented faculty (overcome through phased coaching)
2. Student acclimatization to peer evaluation processes (typically requiring 3-4 TBL sessions)

COVID-19 adaptations complicated TBL delivery but revealed a critical insight: **virtual TBL sessions maintained 91% of in-person outcomes** when using conditional solution software like "scratch film" ([Rethinking active learning, 2026](sourcelink)).

## Conclusion
Despite significant implementation barriers, TBL demonstrates clear comparative advantages for undergraduate dental education according to the reviewed literature:

- **Academic Performance**: TBL generates statistically superior outcomes in applied clinical skills (d=0.42) and complex decision-making, with mixed outcomes for basic science recall compared to flipped classrooms. Performance advantages peak when combined with digital pre-session preparation.
  
- **Student Satisfaction**: TBL consistently outperforms lectures (ES=1.13) and competes favorably with PBL/CBL approaches, particularly regarding clinical relevance perceptions and team skills development.
  
- **Contextual Optimization**: Effectiveness is maximized within **hybrid models** (such as flipped-TBL integration) and requires **systematic institutional support**—including faculty development, space redesign, and AI-enhanced content delivery.

The evidence suggests TBL should not categorically replace all dental pedagogies but rather serve as: 
- A primary modality for technique-intensive courses (operative dentistry, local anesthesia)
- A complementary approach alongside PBL/CBL for integrated diagnosis courses
- A transitional bridge from preclinical to clinical training phases

Longitudinal, multi-institutional studies addressing generational differences and artificial intelligence integration represent necessary future research to refine TBL implementation in evolving dental curricula.
